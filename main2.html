About Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad, the largest city in Gujarat, stands as a testament to India's rich heritage and rapid modernization. Known as the "Manchester of India" for its textile industry, the city seamlessly blends 600 years of history with cutting-edge innovation.

1411 AD - Foundation

Founded by Sultan Ahmed Shah on the banks of River Sabarmati

1915-1930 - Gandhi Era

Sabarmati Ashram became the epicenter of India's independence movement

2017 - UNESCO Recognition

First Indian city to be declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site

Present - Smart City

Leading India's smart city transformation with digital infrastructure

Must-Visit Attractions

Sabarmati Ashram

Historic residence of Mahatma Gandhi, now a museum showcasing the freedom struggle. The ashram attracts over 1 million visitors annually.

Historical Museum

Akshardham Temple

A magnificent temple complex showcasing Indian culture, spirituality, and architecture. Features exhibitions, garden, and water shows.

Spiritual Modern

Adalaj Stepwell

An architectural marvel from 1499, this five-story stepwell features intricate carvings and served as a resting place for travelers.

Architecture 15th Century

Jama Masjid

Built in 1424, this grand mosque is one of the most splendid mosques in India, known for its Indo-Islamic architecture.

Religious Indo-Islamic

Kite Museum

World's largest collection of kites, showcasing the art and culture of kite flying. Perfect representation of Gujarat's kite festival tradition.

Cultural Unique

Sabarmati Riverfront

11-km long waterfront development project featuring gardens, promenades, and recreational facilities along the Sabarmati River.

Recreation Modern

Smart Transportation

Ahmedabad Metro

Modern metro rail system connecting major areas of the city with 38 stations across two lines.

  • North-South Corridor
  • East-West Corridor

India's first Bus Rapid Transit System with dedicated lanes covering 85+ km across the city.

  • 180+ bus stations
  • Dedicated bus lanes
  • Environment friendly
