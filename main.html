About Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad, the largest city in Gujarat, stands as a testament to India's rich heritage and rapid modernization. Known as the "Manchester of India" for its textile industry, the city seamlessly blends 600 years of history with cutting-edge innovation.

Royal Heritage

Once the capital of Gujarat Sultanate, Ahmedabad has been ruled by various dynasties, each leaving their architectural and cultural imprint on the city.

Industrial Legacy

The city's textile mills earned it the nickname "Manchester of India" and made it the commercial hub of western India.

1411 AD - Foundation

Founded by Sultan Ahmed Shah on the banks of River Sabarmati, named after the founder himself.

1915-1930 - Gandhi Era

Sabarmati Ashram became the epicenter of India's independence movement under Mahatma Gandhi's leadership.

1960 - Capital City

Became the capital of newly formed Gujarat state before the capital moved to Gandhinagar in 1970.

2017 - UNESCO Recognition

First Indian city to be declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its rich architectural heritage.

Present - Smart City

Leading India's smart city transformation with digital infrastructure and sustainable development.
